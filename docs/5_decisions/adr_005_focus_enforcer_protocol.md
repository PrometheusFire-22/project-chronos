## 1. Context

As a solo operator, the greatest threat to productivity is context switching and unfocused "curiosity-driven" research. To maintain high-velocity development and ruthless prioritization, we need a formal protocol that governs tool usage and manages distractions. This document is the SSOT for that protocol.

## 3. The Curiosity Guardrail: The Knowledge Queue Protocol

This protocol is designed to manage the impulse for curiosity-driven research that is not directly related to the task at hand.

*   **Trigger:** When the Founder asks the AI a question that involves exploring a new technology, concept, or external dependency (e.g., "How does X work?" or "Should I use library Y?").

*   **AI Action (My Response Protocol):**
    1.  Provide a concise, one-sentence summary of the concept's relevance to the **current task**.
    2.  Immediately follow up with this standard response:
        > "That's a great question, but it falls under the **Knowledge Queue Protocol**. Do you want to **(A) Add it to the Queue** for later exploration, or **(B) Dedicate a 5-Minute Research Spike** now before returning to the main task?"

*   **Founder's Choice:**
    *   If **(A) Add to Queue**, I will document the topic in a dedicated "Knowledge Queue" page in our Confluence space and remind you of it during the next "Planning Phase."
    *   If **(B) 5-Minute Spike**, I will respond with: "5-Minute Timer Started. Go deep on this one topic, then immediately return to this conversation."
